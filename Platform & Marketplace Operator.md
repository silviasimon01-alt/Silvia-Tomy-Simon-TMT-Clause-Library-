## Drafting Recommendations & Practical Controls

**Must-have (High Risk):**
- Robust notice-and-takedown mechanism with timelines, counter-notice, record retention and appeal channel.
- KYC/KYB onboarding checklists, sanctions screening and AML escalation flows.
- Escrow/nodal-account provisions, daily reconciliation, proof-of-funds and settlement run-books.
- Clear consumer-facing terms: pre-contractual disclosures, returns policy, statutorily-mandated cooling-off rights.

**Recommended (Medium Risk):**
- Transparency clause for ranking and sponsored content; algorithmic explainability summary for affected users where required.
- Data portability & export templates for departing Sellers (machine-readable CSV/JSON formats).
- Developer program security controls, API rate-limits and key-revocation rights.

**Standard (Low Risk):**
- SLAs for API uptime, listings availability, and settlement SLAs with credits for breach.
- Insurance minimums and certificate delivery timelines.
- Source-code escrow for proprietary marketplace components that Sellers rely on.

## Final Note (Operational & Legal Integration Guidance)

1. **Clarify Platform Role early.** Whether the Platform is an intermediary, principal/reseller or hybrid determines regulatory exposure (payments, consumer claims, product liability).  
2. **Make licences & compliance a condition precedent.** Do not permit onboarding or go-live until KYB/KYC, payment integrations and escrow confirmations are in place.  
3. **Prioritise transparency & auditable logs.** Keep immutable logs for takedown actions, fund movements and seller onboarding steps to support regulatory defence.  
4. **Avoid over-broad disclaimers for consumers.** Consumer rights and data-protection obligations cannot be entirely contracted away ‚Äî carve-outs for statutory rights must be explicit.  
5. **Governance cadence.** Quarterly compliance attestations, annual independent SOC 2/ISO audits, and real-time fraud monitoring are industry-standard controls.  
6. **Localise per market.** Tax (marketplace facilitator), payments (nodal/escrow) and labour classification rules vary widely ‚Äî include exhibit per jurisdiction.
