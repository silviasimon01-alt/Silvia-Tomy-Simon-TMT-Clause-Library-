## Drafting Recommendations & Practical Controls  

**Must-have (High Risk):**  
- Data privacy addendum for minors (COPPA / GDPR-K / DPDP compliance).  
- Monetisation disclosure templates and refund policy in consumer-facing terms.  
- Tournament integrity charter + anti-cheat enforcement policy.  
- Explicit IP assignment + separate engine license acknowledgment.  

**Recommended (Medium Risk):**  
- Parental consent workflow and DPA for player data collection.  
- Audit trail for streaming analytics and revenue-share verification.  
- Player/team standard contract template with arbitration clause.  

**Standard (Low Risk):**  
- Force majeure, standard liability caps, confidentiality, and indemnity.

## Final Note (Operational & Legal Integration Guidance)  

1. **Differentiate regulatory zones:** Game, esports, and gambling overlap‚Äîconduct a legality check per jurisdiction.  
2. **Privacy-by-design:** Build consent, parental verification, and opt-out systems early in development.  
3. **Balance publisher‚Äìdeveloper IP rights:** Publishers need exploitation rights; developers retain tools and tech.  
4. **Platform dependence:** Integrate compliance with Steam, Xbox, PSN, and Apple/Google distribution rules.  
5. **Esports integrity:** Include anti-cheat, anti-fraud, and match-fixing safeguards tied to player agreements.  
6. **Moderation = liability shield:** Maintain documented takedown workflows to preserve safe-harbor protection.  
7. **Revenue audits & transparency:** Ensure clear revenue-share logic for teams, streamers, and tournaments.
